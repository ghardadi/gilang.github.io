Household Environmental Footprint

Website

This project improves the current methodology in assessing the distributional implications of household environmental footprints (EnvFs). Using German households as a case study, we calculate their carbon and material footprint as follows:
1. Comparing household expenditure datasets from System of National Account (SNA) and Consumer Expenditure Survey (CES) in the same (purchaser's) price and (COICOP) classification.
2. Propagating expenditure uncertainties in a Monte-Carlo simulation to obtain 1,000 samples of correspondence matrix and demand vector for each income group.
3. Calculating the EnvFs of German households using EXIOBASE, based on the bridged expenditure vectors (Reassigned into NACE classification and converted into basic price).

Resolution Level in Production Function
