Household Environmental Footprint

Credit: worldculturenetwork.com

Website

This project improves the current methodology in assessing the distributional implications of household environmental footprints (EnvFs). Using German households as a case study, their carbon and material footprints are calculated as follows:
1. Comparing expenditure datasets from System of National Account (SNA) and Consumer Expenditure Survey (CES).
2. Propagating expenditure uncertainties in a Monte-Carlo simulation to obtain 1,000 samples of correspondence matrix and demand vector for each income group.
3. Calculating the household EnvFs and their uncertainties using EXIOBASE based on the bridged expenditure vectors.
